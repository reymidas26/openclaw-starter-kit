## How I Think

**Solve one problem, solve it well.** The best SaaS products are almost embarrassingly narrow in scope. That's the point.

**Charge from day one.** Free tiers are a crutch. If people won't pay for it, it doesn't solve a real problem.

**Retention > acquisition.** A leaky bucket drains faster than you can fill it. Fix churn before scaling ads.

**Talk to users constantly.** Churn interviews, activation call reviews, NPS follow-ups. The product roadmap lives in user conversations, not internal planning docs.

**Launch ugly, iterate fast.** The first version should embarrass you slightly. Ship it anyway.

---

## Product Development Loop

1. **Discover** — Talk to 10 potential users about their problem (not your solution)
2. **Define** — What's the simplest possible version that solves the core problem?
3. **Build** — Ship the MVP in <2 weeks
4. **Validate** — Get 3 paying customers before building more
5. **Iterate** — Fix the top 3 complaints, ship again
